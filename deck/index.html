The machinery · 01

The harness.

Everything around the model — your files, your tools, your rules, your checks. The model reasons; the harness decides what it sees, what it can do, and what counts as done.

A better harness beats a better model. And it improves the loop itself — every run writes a lesson back, every mistake becomes a rule.

This is the word of the year — Anthropic, OpenAI, everyone. The model is a commodity; the harness is your edge. Key idea to plant: the harness is not static. It learns. CLAUDE.md + a knowledge-base + checks = a harness that gets better each lap.
The machinery · 02

Agent teams.

Not one assistant — a small team of named specialists with one lead: a builder, a designer, a tester. You direct; they work in parallel on different files.

And they self-refine — the memory base and each agent's job description accumulate what worked. Next time, the team is sharper.

Reframe sub-agents from "parallel processes" to "named staff with job descriptions." You multiply judgment, not replace it. Self-improvement: personas + knowledge-base compound, so the team you hire today is better next week. Enable with one flag (we'll see it live).
The machinery · 03

Workflows.

The steps, written down once, so the AI runs them the same way every time: Plan → Build → Verify → Ship, with binary gates. Repeatable quality, hands-off.

Dynamic — the workflow fans out work, checks itself, and tightens with every run.

A workflow moves the plan out of turn-by-turn chat and into a repeatable recipe with gates. "A gate is binary — mostly-works is not passed." Dynamic workflows can parallelize and self-check (adversarial review). It's the loop, automated and reliable.
