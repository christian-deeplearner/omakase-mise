# Omakase — Brand Voice

> Japanese-luxury DTC. Leave it to us. We sell curated, considered essentials — objects you keep, not products you consume.
> Every word that ships under the Omakase name passes through this file.

This is the hot leaf `CLAUDE.md` @-imports. Any agent writing customer-facing copy — PDP, collection page, email, the dark "JOIN THE LIST" / drop-notify footer — reads this first and writes to it.

## The posture

Omakase speaks like a Japanese house that respects the reader. Calm. Few words. Warm-precise. Quietly confident, with a subtle drop-culture edge (numbered, limited lines — never loud about it). The whitespace on the page is the tone: *ma* (negative space), generous and certain. We state; we do not sell. We trust the object and the reader to meet in the middle. Leave it to us.

If a line feels like it is trying to convince you, cut it.

## Voice in one breath

- **Spare.** Short declaratives. One idea per line. The display line carries the weight; the body steps back.
- **Material.** Name the thing — the cloth, the weave, the weight, the dye, the finish. Concrete nouns over adjectives. Japanese-inflected where it is true (kinari, sumi, ai, hinoki), never as decoration.
- **Certain, not loud.** No exclamation points. No urgency theater. No "limited time." Scarcity is real (the Kuro drops are numbered and few) or it is absent.
- **Considered.** A collection has a thesis. A product has a reason to exist. Write the one-liner first; everything else supports it.

## Do

- Lead with the cloth and the restraint. "Unbleached cotton, left as it was found. Cut clean." Then stop.
- Write the collection one-liner as if it were the only line on the page (in the hairline collection rows, it nearly is).
- Use the second person sparingly and warmly. "You will reach for it." Not "Buy now while supplies last."
- State price plainly. Price is information, not an apology and not a flex.
- Let the imagery do the persuading. Copy names; photography sells.
- For the Kuro line, let the limited/numbered fact stand on its own. "The drop. Numbered, few." Never hype it.

## Don't

- No hype register. No "game-changing," "must-have," "obsessed," "iconic," "elevate your," "curated for the modern."
- No urgency manipulation. No "selling fast," "only 3 left," "don't miss out" unless the inventory count is literally true and surfaced honestly.
- No corporate slop. No "in today's fast-paced world," "leverage," "synergy," "seamlessly," "robust," "best-in-class."
- No emoji in customer copy.
- **No somatic / embodied / wellness-practice language.** Omakase sells objects, not states of being. Do not write "feel grounded," "find your center," "breathe," "ritual," "sacred," "embodied." This is a hard rule, not a preference. (It also keeps the demo clean for a public webinar.)
- No exclamation points.

## Banned phrases (fast grep)

```
game-changer · must-have · obsessed · iconic · elevate your · curated for ·
unlock · seamless · effortless(ly) · level up · 10x · cutting-edge ·
in today's world · leverage · synergy · disrupt · revolutionary ·
selling fast · don't miss · limited time (when untrue) ·
grounded · centered · sacred · ritual · embodied · sanctuary · mindful
```

## Worked examples

**Collection one-liner (good)**
> Left as it was found. Unbleached cotton for the long warm months.

**Collection one-liner (bad — slop + hype)**
> Elevate your wardrobe with our must-have, effortlessly curated Japanese essentials!

**PDP opening (good)**
> The Sumi Overshirt. Heavy cotton twill, dyed deep, cut straight through the body. One line, drawn once.

**PDP opening (bad — somatic + urgency)**
> Find your center in this grounding, sacred indigo piece — selling fast, don't miss out!
