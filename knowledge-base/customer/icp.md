# Omakase — Ideal Customer Profile

> Who we build for. When an agent writes a PDP, designs a page, or scores a collection, it writes for this person — not for "everyone."

Omakase is a fictional, fake-data demo brand. This ICP is seeded, internally consistent, and deliberately specific. Specificity is the point: a legible customer is a customer agents can write for.

## The one we build for

**Akiko, 36.** Lives in a quiet mid-rise in a coastal city. Design director at a studio she does not name at parties. Owns twelve things she truly loves and replaces them slowly. Reads the cloth and the construction before the price tag. Has unsubscribed from every brand that emails her twice a week. Knows what *kinari* and *sumi* mean and notices when a brand uses them carelessly.

She does not want more. She wants *better, and fewer*. She will pay for the cotton that washes well for ten years and the indigo that ages instead of fading. She will not be rushed, flattered, or alarmed into a purchase. She trusts a house that says, plainly, *leave it to us*.

## What she values

| Value | What it means for our copy and UI |
|---|---|
| **Material honesty** | Name the cloth, the weight, the dye, the origin. She checks. |
| **Longevity** | "It ages / it patinas / it lasts" beats "trending now." |
| **Restraint (*ma*)** | Whitespace, one product per view, no carousel of upsells. |
| **Quiet** | No popups, no countdown timers, no "you left something behind" guilt. |
| **Considered curation** | A collection with a thesis reads as a point of view, not a catalog. The chef's selection. |
| **Earned scarcity** | The numbered Kuro drops are few and stated plainly — never hyped. |

## What turns her away (anti-patterns)

- Urgency theater ("only 2 left!", flashing timers).
- Discount-driven identity ("40% off everything!"). Omakase discounts rarely and quietly, or not at all.
- Over-personalization that feels like surveillance.
- Slop copy. She can smell a thesaurus from the homepage.
- Wellness-practice language pasted onto a garment. She buys clothes, not states of being.

## How she shops

- Arrives from a saved link or a friend, not an ad she half-remembers.
- Reads the collection page top to bottom. The one-liner decides whether she scrolls.
- Reads the full PDP. Material, care, fit, returns — all of it.
- Adds one thing. Maybe two. Checks out without an account if she can.
- Joins the list for the Kuro drops — and expects to be contacted once, well.
- Comes back in a season, not a week.

## Implications for the build

- **Storefront:** one product per view, full-bleed imagery, the PDP carries real material/care/fit copy (not lorem). The collection one-liner is load-bearing. The six collections read as a tasting menu (glyph · name · tagline · →).
- **Command center:** her order is high-AOV, low-frequency. Operator metrics that matter: AOV, repeat-in-season rate, return rate, drop sell-through — not raw volume.
- **Voice:** see `../voice/brand-voice.md`. Akiko is exactly the reader the banned-phrase list protects.

## Honesty note

Akiko is seeded, not real. No real customer data lives in this repo. The point of a sharp fictional ICP is the same as the point of a sharp real one: it makes the work legible enough that an agent can do it well.
